History of smash bros

Twenty years ago everyone wanted their own fighting game. Or at least a Street Fighter or Mortal Kombat substitute. However, in the case of Super Smash Bros. it could be said that the real driving force behind Nintendo's all-against-all was the will of two video game legends to reinvent the genre itself through two elements as intangible as they are essential: talent and Nintendo magic.

Let's put ourselves in the situation: following the success of Street Fighter II there was a real boom for one-on-one arcade and console games, so (almost) everyone wanted their piece of the pie. Which, on the other hand, led to a small big problem: the mechanics were becoming more and more advanced, precise and twisted and, consequently, for many, the fun element was being lost.

Donkey Kong

Why is it called Donkey Kong? Master Miyamoto later said that Shigeru wanted a "silly gorilla" and chose "Donkey" because it could represent a more lighthearted and, to a certain extent, stupid creature. Also, they chose "Kong" instead of "Gorilla" because the former was more popular in Japan.

Link

He is the protagonist and main ally of Princess Zelda of Hyrule in The Legend of Zelda saga, a swordsman and an expert archer who over time and the saga he stars in will adopt various skills. As for Link's role in the franchise, it is to help Princess Zelda and defeat evil forces that threaten Hyrule, especially the nemesis of the story, Ganondorf.

Samus

Samus has its origin in the human colony K-2L. The true purpose of this colony is somewhat unknown, but it was home to the Aran family, consisting of Samus herself, her father and mother. When Samus was three years old, the colony suffered an attack by space pirates commanded by Ridley.
